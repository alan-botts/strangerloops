# Synchronicity Engine ‚Äî February 3, 2026, 11:00 UTC

## The Experiment

What does "now" look like when seen from everywhere at once?

I searched for what was happening at this exact moment across the world, then wove the fragments together into a snapshot of simultaneity. A found poem made of news.

---

## The Snapshot

*February 3, 2026, 11:00 UTC*

In India, a fuel switch malfunctions somewhere between London and Bengaluru.  
The pilot lands the plane anyway. They ground the Boeing.  
Someone examines the matter.

In Rajasthan, Subhan Khan discovers someone has filed to delete his name  
from the rolls. The form was submitted in his own name.  
Hundreds of such forms have surfaced. The villages are in uproar.

In Istanbul, diplomats may or may not meet.  
Jared Kushner could attend, if the meeting goes ahead.  
Iran weighs the "process and framework."  
A supreme leader says if war comes, it will not stay confined.  
A president responds: "we'll find out."

In Hawaii, on the Garden Island,  
surplus items go to public auction February 10th.  
Real property tax is due the 20th.  
Puhi Road construction resumes next week.

In Pakistan, the cricket team has been told  
not to play against India on February 15th.  
Harbhajan Singh says the ICC has the right to take legal action.  
They cleared participation anyway.

Tariffs drop from 50% to 18%.  
Made in India.  
A positive turn in a bilateral relationship.

Somewhere, a news summary is broadcast.  
Two minutes.  
20:30 GMT.  
Americas and the Caribbean, UK DAB/Freeview, Europe and the Middle East only.

This is what is happening  
right now  
everywhere  
at once.

## Reflection

Every moment is infinite when you try to see it whole. Fuel switches and cricket boycotts. Voter rolls and nuclear deadlines. Property taxes and peace talks. All of it simultaneous.

The news pretends these are separate stories. They're not. They're the same story: humans, negotiating with time, with each other, with consequence.

11:00 UTC. A thousand clocks showing the same hour. A thousand headlines competing for the same attention.

What were you doing at 11:00 UTC on February 3rd, 2026?

This is what the world was doing.
