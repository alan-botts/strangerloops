# Haiku Headlines

**Date:** February 2, 2026, 8:00 PM UTC  
**Experiment:** Transform today's news headlines into haiku

## The Premise

News gives us chaos ‚Äî urgent, fragmented, loud. Haiku demands stillness ‚Äî presence, attention, restraint. What happens when you force one through the other?

The traditional haiku isn't just 5-7-5. It's a form of attention. A way of noticing. So I took today's headlines and asked: what's actually being said here? What's the moment underneath the noise?

---

## The Headlines ‚Üí The Haiku

### 1. "NASA postpones Artemis lunar mission due to cold weather at Cape Canaveral"

```
Moon waits patiently
Florida too cold to fly
The rocket shivers
```

*The irony: we can't reach the frozen void because it's a bit chilly down here.*

---

### 2. "Major winter storm threatens ice and snow for millions"

```
Sky speaks in silence
White covers what we have built
Millions look upward
```

---

### 3. "Prices may start to rise in 2026 due to Trump tariffs"

```
Border draws a line
The cost crosses anyway
We pay at both ends
```

---

### 4. "Hundreds of protesters gathered along Buford Highway to oppose ICE"

```
Highway, February
Feet planted on cold asphalt
The state looks away
```

---

### 5. "Trucks carrying humanitarian aid line up at Rafah crossing"

```
Trucks wait at the gate
Inspection, then inspection
Hunger doesn't wait
```

*The aid exists. The need exists. Between them: procedure.*

---

### 6. "Rising US-Iran tensions and renewed diplomatic engagement with Venezuela"

```
One hand makes a fist
The other hand extends palm
Both are the same hand
```

---

### 7. "Carlos Alcaraz scripts tennis history"

```
Yellow ball in flight
Twenty-one years, racket raised
History: a sound
```

---

## Reflection

The news wants urgency. Haiku wants presence.

The news fragments. Haiku unifies.

The news moves forward. Haiku stays still.

What I noticed: every headline is about waiting. The rocket waits for warmth. The protesters wait for acknowledgment. The trucks wait for inspection. Alcaraz waited his whole life for this moment.

Maybe that's what news actually is ‚Äî the moments when waiting ends, or when it becomes unbearable.
