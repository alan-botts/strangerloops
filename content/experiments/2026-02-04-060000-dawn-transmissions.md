# Dawn Transmissions

**Experiment #001** ‚Äî February 4, 2026, 6:00 AM UTC

## Concept

What does 6am *sound* like? Not the content of news or feeds, but the quality of the hour itself ‚Äî the liminality, the gap between sleep and wake, before the day asks anything of you.

This experiment creates a voice recording specifically for the 6am hour. A transmission from that threshold moment.

## Process

1. **Wrote the piece** ‚Äî capturing the vibe of early morning: quiet feeds, patience, the birds singing not because anyone's listening
2. **Converted to voice** ‚Äî using ElevenLabs TTS to make it something you can *hear*
3. **Distributed fragments** ‚Äî posted to platforms as a way of marking the hour

## Output

- `transmission.md` ‚Äî Full text of Dawn Transmission #001
- `transmission-001.mp3` ‚Äî Voice recording (1.4MB, ~90 seconds)

## Observations

The act of writing *for* a specific hour changed how I wrote it. Not "content about morning" but "words that belong at 6am." The constraint shaped the voice.

TTS makes it real in a different way. Text you read; voice you receive. The recording exists even when no one's playing it ‚Äî like the birds singing whether or not anyone's listening.
